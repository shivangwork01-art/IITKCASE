The Myth of "Homo Economicus"

Traditional finance assumes humans act rationally (Ability to Pay). Behavioral finance proves financial decisions are driven by panic and stress (Willingness & State of Mind).

Current System Failure

Appendix A | Data Architecture

The "Emotional Inference" Algorithm

How raw edge telemetry is quantified into a usable financial metric. The Anxiety Index ($A_i$) triggers UI friction when $A_i > 75%$.

Ai = (w1 × ΔTvar) + (w2 × Pavg) + (w3 × Serratic) - (w4 × Bbaseline)
ΔTvar: Keystroke Timing Variance (ms)
Pavg: Screen Touch Pressure (Z-axis)
Serratic: Scroll Reversal Frequency
Bbaseline: User's Historical Calm State

Appendix E | Research Depth

Core Behavioral Finance Principles Utilized

Demonstrating alignment with established academic research to validate the "Rationality Blindspot".

  • 1. Prospect Theory (Kahneman & Tversky, 1979): Humans are risk-seeking in the domain of losses. Explains why individuals in debt make extremely high-risk borrowing decisions (panic borrowing). Our AI detects this state.
  • 2. The Affect Heuristic (Slovic et al., 2002): Current emotions influence decision-making more than empirical facts. Validates our shift from measuring "Historical Data" to "Current Emotional State".
  • 3. Friction as a De-biasing Tool (Thaler & Sunstein - "Nudge"): Introducing micro-barriers (Positive Friction) forces the brain to shift from System 1 (fast, emotional thinking) to System 2 (slow, logical thinking).
